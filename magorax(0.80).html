MAGORAX





Atop the irregular and challenging terrain of a craggy hill, beneath the domain of a solitary giant oak, a formidable figure stands tall, their sparkling eyes keenly overlooking a corpse-strewn battlefield as shade fades and grim deathmists dissipate.

The first light of dawn breaks through the clouds and gently kisses the figure’s black war-paint smeared face: Sturdy jaw, like chiseled stone; deep-blue chin-beard, braided and bloodstained.

His bare hairless chest heaves, exhausted, but his loins remain girded.


These loins belong to I, Magorax


I, Magorax, survey the spattered battlefield upon which I am the last standing.

Smoke still rises from smoldering embers. The ground is littered with the broken bodies of soldiers, their blood soaking the earth and staining it a sickly shade.

Adjusting stance, my four lightly armored legs trample the pristine satiny-green grasses beneath my hooves. My tail flicks as the morning breeze tickles my bright-purple hide-hair. My powerful hearts pound as though I were at the height of battle, and yet-


Nothing but me stirs



I, Magorax, am a Centaur[99:M:BattleMaster]. My speed, ranged weaponry, and carrying capacity, bestow a tactical edge. Additionally, I’m eight feet tall and sport a two-foot-long dong. My hefty peen and scrote are suspended underneath, horsey-style, safely between my back legs, as opposed to precariously dangling up front; a design decision that would be an aesthetic—not to mention strategic—failure.

My strat? Intimidate on a pre-conscious primal level. Decisive, elegant, aggressive, and dominant; commanding respect from allies and enemies both; I, Magorax, am an Alpha.

This world belongs to me.


Why then do I, Magorax, throb to the beat of fear?


Beneath the rhythms of my excited hearts, silence saturates.

Memories of past victories. Only a select few remain distinct. Details faded like old scars, but emotions fresh like open wounds. Those victories feel different. Today, there is an emptiness.


This game is not yet over


Cantering, I, Magorax, circle the giant oak clockwise. I scan the horizon for movement.

To the East, the Sun executes its rising routine, embracing the plains with warmth. Light glints off of discarded weapons and armor casting suspect shadows on the ground.

North, to my rear, an impenetrable forest. West and South, lay the sorry husks of the slain.

I pause my breathing; my chest stops heaving. I glance up into the still leaves of the tree above, then back out over the battlefield.

I listen deeply—the gentle morning breeze seems to have ceased.


A disembodied voice warns,

Danger


I, Magorax, flinch—startled by the ethereal sound of the distant, and yet very present voice. My every purple hair stands at alert. Indeed, the voice is correct; there is a danger: Though I hold the high ground, I am vulnerable.

I circle the tree once more then stop to again look over the battlefield. I grit my teeth and steel myself, eyes straining, observing only stillness. I reach into my custom TheNailLeatherCo FannyPack, then equip my Glass Longbow. From my quiver, I select a Fleetwood-Boggle Arrow.


Crafting items was not a skill-tree I, Magorax, trained. This uniquely crafted projectile was a gift from friends of an even rarer quality. I tap the shaft of the artesian arrow against my glass bow. The impact emits a clack—the clack, clacks-back from the battlefield.


There should be no echo


“What are you waiting for?” I bellow. Rearing up, I kick out my front legs and bray, “Come at me, brrrrwuuugh!” The impromptu taunt, though intimidatingly delivered, rings hollow. Out of character.


Only my echo responds.


I follow up with a haughty, “Ha-ha!” as subterfuge, cloaking rising anxiety. My hooves thunder back to the ground, distributing force throughout my sturdy muscular form, my scrote jiggles, and my peen pendulums left and right like meaty clockwork. Blood pumping, the timings of my hearts are comparatively irregular.


Irregular like the echo


At last, I, Magorax, make sense of what my exquisitely sensitive body is telling me.

“We’ll play it your way,” I mutter, drawing back my bowstring. I aim at the sky above the battlefield and exert maximum pull. Sweat trickles down my nose as I hold One-Hundred-Fifty units of bow-force.

Nervously, I thumb the arrow’s soft red cockatrice-feather fletching, giving my cowardly unseen enemy a couple more seconds to make their move.


My Fleetwood Boggle Arrow has a specific purpose: At apex, the red cockatrice feathers guiding its flight will effect a fiery explosion spreading Boggle dust over the greatest possible range, negating any active magic.

I’m eager to see its effect. With all magic negated, my battle master skills and ridiculous stats shall give me the edge over all but the strongest opponents. But surely my hearts do not drum this fiercely with mere anticipation. The inner truth of my excitement, a smouldering fear of the unknown.


Ceasing to hold back, I neigh, “Die like a coward then!”

The bow sounds a thwonk the fleetwood flies.


I, Magorax, count aloud, “Eighteen. Seventeen. Sixteen,” meanwhile, producing three silver arrows. Straining to look into the middle-distance, my estimate of my echo’s point of reflection, I rest the arrows between each of the fingers on my left hand.


My hearts thrash spastically as my count continues, “…ten. Nine.”


The familiar ethereal feminine voice repeats,

Danger


The voice’s volume increases, conveying a sort of vaguely urgent concern. I, Magorax, ignore the voice and complete my count, “…two. One. Zero.”


Far overhead, the arrow combusts. A shimmering parabolic umbra descends upon the entire battlefield like a skirt of glitter.

Thirty paces South-East, my echo’s point of reflection, magical sparks illuminate the surface of a previously invisible monolith. Towering like a parapet; much larger than any enemy I, Magorax, can defeat with mere arrows and a greatsword.


Bly-scnharr! It’s huge!


The obscured object unfolds, mechanically roaring and twanging as it attunes its orientation to fix on me. Its clanking carapace glimmers with a cold electric sizzle. The object’s barely visible surface shatters into scale-like shielding that shifts backward in waves while I, Magorax, stand motionless.


What… is… it?


As if to answer my thought the juggernaut spins toward me tearing up the ground between us. It surges through the battlefield, obliterating everything in its path.

Momentarily mesmerized by this thundering thingamabob, I, Magorax, attempt to comprehend it as it shifts shape.

Flashing metallic coils spring outward, whirling and whanging; like glittering tendrils. Its shape cycles: now sharp, twisting, and rolling. The mass roars and hisses as it somehow spirals forward into itself transmuting the discernible into a spectral nothingness.

Unable to focus on it; it evades perception as though refringent through a dark crystal. Its form; amorphous and confusing.

Indecipherable.


Something… not of this world


Closer, its constantly warping form morphs into a rippling maelstrom of blades. Whatever-it-is, shifts more rapidly than my eyes can track, other than to know it’s moving toward me.


Too fast


From the aether, the lady-voice irritatingly calls out for a third time,

Danger!


Danger, indeed


This is no magic I’ve ever seen. It cannot be magic; my boggle dust has seen to it. Only five seconds ago, the arrow had detonated. Already, this beast is within twenty paces.


Too close


My powerful highly-trained muscles spring into action. Galloping, I put the tree between myself and the advancing cloud of blooming metal. My hurried pace propelled by a certainty that any further delay will introduce me to my oblivion.

Employing the most powerful tactic of all—surprise—I command, “Fire!”


The air disappears in an ear-splitting flash, erupting from the oak branches above. The incredible blast projects into the direct center of the indefinable distortion. That blast, is immediately followed by a much larger secondary impact explosion.


The shockwave hits I, Magorax. My torso is partially protected by the tree’s trunk, but my hindquarters are pulverized; vital connective tissues temporarily liquefied. All four of my legs snap beneath; bones crack and joints pop. My face and dick hit the dirt simultaneously, at such speed that I, Magorax don't feel the pain.


The distorted beast disintegrates with a vaporous *plip*. Its sizzling, hissing, clanging, and whirling cease—the only sound remaining is the high-pitched squeal of my perforated eardrums.


I, Magorax, regain focus on the sky above me; smoke clearing as ash particles of 10,000 vaporized leaves drift. The mighty oak laid bare, bark blasted away. Within the scorched branches: Two goblins and a spent KarkNarBoomCo Shock Cannon.


The goblins descend from the tree like heavily armored squirrels. They remove their metal gloves and helmets as they scramble toward me.

I look up at the goblins; their charred armor smoldering and sparking. I can’t hear them, but their thin gobliny lips move in unison. They join hands, attempting to cast a healing spell.


Grelnak[43:M:Warlock], and Sleenklur[36:F:Berzerker], are my allies.

Fellow adventurers.

More than that, they are my friends.


Grelnak leads the chant. A stout 3'2", 110lbs. Like all goblins in the World of Pathena, he has gold-green eyes and red skin, horizontally striped with handsome camouflage of an even deeper red hue. Grelnak sports a mustache, hanging below his nose like an extravagantly bristled broom, protected from the elements by a custom bronze guard, anchored to a piercing through his septum. A sparse crown of unprotected black hair surrounds his battle-scarred dome.

Especially fierce in his youth, his scars were souvenirs of skirmishes, before he was adopted by brownies and taught the benefits to tempering aggressive impulses. Controlled temperament permits greater concentration, and all the bonuses, buffs, and perks that brings.

Adult goblins under thirty are uncommonly strong for creatures of their size, but so too are they uncommonly impulsive and destructive. Older than sixty, and they are wise enough to master spell-casting, but entirely too weak to engage in melee combat.

Being a middle-level goblin, Grelnak occupies the sweet spot between strong-young-dumb, and weak-old-wise. His warlock abilities have saved my hide on countless campaigns.


Joining in Grelnak’s chant, is his berzerker love-mate, Sleenklur. Her intensity augments any experience.

Standing 3'5", and 90 pounds, Sleenklur is leaner and stronger than Grelnak. This is partly due to age, and partly to upbringing. She hails from a warrior clan of mountain goblins. Typical for her clan, her skin is a pinker shade of red, displaying a cute pattern of banded stripes.

Sleenklur’s cuteness cloaks an explosive temper. If agitated enough, red steam shoots out of her ears. She’s a berserker—and not just in battle.

On one occasion, I, Magorax, had the pleasure to witness a drunken rogue pull back a stump after getting grabby. Hard liquor and Sleenklur’s full goblin mode is a combustible combo.


Managing the emotional states of goblins is a great skill to have. Grelnak uniquely possesses the ability to de-escalate Sleenklur should anything get her blood boiling. And I, Magorax, am talented at elevating both of their moods.

Entertaining each other with hilarious jokes and fantastic tales. Helping when in need; united against common enemies. Journeying together; experiencing epic escapades and ecstasies.

Their weird bodies, proportioned in slightly different ratios. Tight, tough skin, stretched over even tighter and tougher muscle. An aesthetically pleasing highly evolved form; though they be tiny, they're mature and powerful. Something about their lack of nipples entangles the eye in a pleasant way. A tiny subversion of mammalian expectation. Looking at them fills one with an impressed feeling, as though looking at a magnificent artwork.

But also, they possess a sort of sickliness compelling compassion, filling one with an urge to care for them. To accept the dares and challenges of navigating potentially explosive situations. Willingly exposing oneself to potentially harmful experiences, while trusting any connection you build in the moment is worth more than any bridge you might burn in the future.

A Goblins’ charm comes from this perceived risk; the peak experience that lay betwixt the depths of danger and desire.

Sleenklur, Grelnak, and I, Magorax, are bonded to one another as only true adventurers can be.



I, Magorax, gaze upon my two goblin comrades chanting over me. Grelnak emotes a frown of concern. In all our adventures, this is the first time I have seen him from this vantage point. Looking up at him, my vision blurs then again refocuses on the glittering tip of my nose. Tiny magical sparks continue to dance around Grelnak’s enchanted blast armor.


As boggle dust settles all over my formerly full-health body, pain finally registers accompanied by intense nausea. I inhale a sweet bloody breath seasoned by my singed hairs, and reluctantly accept reality.


I, Magorax, lay broken


The distant ethereal voice—somehow everywhere—again calls out with an air of impatience,

DANGER!


No shit


With subsequent breaths my battered chest inflates less and less.


Why, though deafened, do I hear this danger-voice?

What is this strange beast that has felled me?

And, what is the answer to the Enigma of Chrome?


These things—I, Magorax—will never know. My boggle dust has seen to it; negating my goblin friends’ healing magic.


My hearts cease their pounding and I exhale my final painful breath. Grelnak’s gold and green eyes meet mine; a shared look, conveying all that needs to be said. Below his ornate bronze stash armor, his gobliny lips grimace in sad realization of the truth of the moment.


Sleenklur, Grelnak, and I, Magorax: Countless adventures. Innumerable triumphs, like the time we led the distraction raid for the Alliance of the Western Plains, and were so successful we opened up a whole new front, a decisive part of ending the second Eternal War. Or the time we rescued the heir to the Southern Throne, accepted the reward, then blew it all on pixie dust.

Together, we lived life; and now, together, we meet my death.



Before closing my eyes for the last time, I receive the rarest gift of all; the rainbow tinted tears of great goblin friends.


Then, there is nothing.


Not even darkness.


No—there is still an empty pain. A vessel for a trickle of thought and its thinker to pour in. A narrative stream emerges and existence surges. A billion hexagonal bubbles of light focus on a wave in the abyssal distance.

The wave curls and crashes into a frothing foam of hexagons. My experiences linger like a burning phosphor, then submerge in darkness.

A salty taste.

A sizzle.

A plip.


Atop my existence, another overlays itself—it had been there all along.


This has happened before


I, Magorax, do not resist.

I'm calm as though returning home.

I embrace another layer of existence.

My story ends, but I become a part of something bigger.
